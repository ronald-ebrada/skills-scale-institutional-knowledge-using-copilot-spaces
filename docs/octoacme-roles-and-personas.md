# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
The Scrum Master is a servant-leader who facilitates agile ceremonies, removes impediments, and champions continuous improvement. They ensure the team adheres to agile principles and operates at peak efficiency.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint ceremonies: planning, daily standups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove blockers that impede team progress
- Champion agile principles and coach the team on best practices
- Shield the team from external disruptions during sprints
- Support continuous improvement initiatives and track action items

### Goals
- Enable a high-performing, self-organizing team
- Reduce impediments and increase team velocity
- Foster a culture of transparency and continuous learning

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies facilitation
- Blocker escalation to PM and stakeholders
- Retrospective action items and follow-up tracking

### Interactions
- Collaborates closely with Project Manager, Product Manager, and Developers
- Ensures the team operates efficiently and agile ceremonies are productive

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
The Business Analyst bridges the gap between business stakeholders and the delivery team. They gather, clarify, and document requirements, translating business needs into actionable backlog items.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and clarify requirements through stakeholder interviews and workshops
- Map business needs to technical solutions and backlog items
- Document user stories, acceptance criteria, and process flows
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business requirements
- Maintain traceability between business needs and delivered features

### Goals
- Ensure requirements are clear, complete, and actionable before development begins
- Reduce rework caused by ambiguous or misunderstood requirements
- Bridge communication gaps between business stakeholders and engineering

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder interviews and requirements workshops
- User story refinement sessions with the development team
- Documentation updates and acceptance criteria reviews

### Interactions
- Works closely with Product Manager and business stakeholders to gather and prioritize requirements
- Consults Developers for technical feasibility assessments
- Updates and maintains documentation to keep it current and accurate

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
The UX Designer is responsible for the end-to-end user experience of the product. They create wireframes, prototypes, and conduct usability testing to ensure the product meets user needs and defined experience metrics.

### Responsibilities
- Design wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Conduct user research, usability testing, and synthesize findings
- Define and uphold UX standards and design system guidelines
- Collaborate with developers during implementation to ensure design fidelity
- Measure and iterate on user experience based on feedback and metrics

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and delightful user experiences
- Reduce friction and support user adoption of product features
- Ensure design decisions are grounded in user research and data

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions with Product Manager and Developers
- Usability testing reports and UX metrics summaries
- Prototype walkthroughs and handoff documentation

### Interactions
- Aligns with Product Manager on product goals and user outcomes
- Supports Developers during implementation to ensure design consistency
- Partners with QA to validate that implemented features meet UX acceptance criteria

---

## Customer Support Lead

### Role Summary
The Customer Support Lead acts as the voice of the customer within the project team. They monitor user feedback, escalate issues, and feed customer insights into planning and prioritization to improve the product continuously.

### Responsibilities
- Monitor and triage user feedback, bug reports, and support tickets
- Escalate critical bugs and incidents to the appropriate teams
- Synthesize customer insights and contribute to backlog prioritization
- Coordinate incident resolution and communicate status to affected users
- Contribute to retrospectives with post-release learnings and customer impact data

### Goals
- Minimize customer impact from bugs and incidents
- Ensure customer feedback is systematically incorporated into product planning
- Improve post-release support processes and reduce time-to-resolution

### Typical Communication
- Post-release monitoring reports and incident summaries
- Feedback aggregation and triage shared with Project Manager and Product Manager
- Retrospective contributions focused on customer impact and support trends

### Interactions
- Communicates with Project Manager, Product Manager, QA, and Developers post-release
- Contributes customer insights to sprint planning and backlog grooming
- Participates in retrospectives to surface continuous improvement opportunities
